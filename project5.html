Using Biological Structures to improve strength to infill ration in 3D printing

Biological structures have evolved over millennia to optimize load-bearing and energy absorption capabilities using minimal material. Inspired by these natural designs, this study explores six bio-inspired structural models, including transverse honeycombs, rhombic dodecahedrons, and Wearie Phelan structures, to develop lightweight and efficient alternatives to solid material blocks. Using additive manufacturing, these models were fabricated and tested through simulations and physical experiments. Results revealed that the structures required approximately 40% less material while achieving comparable load-bearing performance under moderate forces. Enhanced energy absorption and impact dampening properties were also observed, with modifications like multi-directional force optimization in honeycomb designs contributing to improved performance. These findings highlight the potential of bio-inspired structures as cost-effective, sustainable solutions for diverse applications, leveraging the precision and scalability of modern additive manufacturing techniques.
